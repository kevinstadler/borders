On most political maps produced in modern times, nation states and other geo-political bodies are shown as contiguous (filled-in) areas that span all the way to where they border their adjacent regions. What such depictions represent is really the theoretical extent of the territory over which a government claims sovereign authority, whether it is actually exercised or not. Questions such as which country or state is "the biggest" are vacuous when large swathes of the claimed area are completely uninhabited and uncontrolled. This map explores an arguably more accurate way of representing countries in social terms, by highlighting only those areas inhabited by people who consider themselves members of the imagined community that is their nation.
